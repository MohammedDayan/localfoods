Wasa Wasa

Our wasawasa is a traditional dish from the northern part of Ghana. It is made from dried yam peelings which have been grounded into flour and steamed. The wasawasa is mostly eaten with spicy sauces and sometimes garnished with vegetables, accompanied with shea butter or raw groundnut oil and fried fish or cow skin. It is sometimes served with beans, spaghetti, and salad The wasawasa is rich in carbohydrates, protein and vitamins

Tuu Bani

This is another dish from the northern part of Ghana. We make it with cowpeas, which are a type of beans. The cowpeas are soaked, blended, and mixed with salt and dawadawa. The mixture is then poured into a bowl and covered with a cloth. It is left to ferment for a few days, until it becomes sour and bubbly. The fermented batter is then steamed in banana leaves or aluminum foil until firm. Tubaani is usually eaten with pepper sauce, which is a spicy sauce made with tomatoes, onions, peppers, and oil. Our specially made tubani provides our customers with nutrients rich in iron, vitamins A, vitamin B1 and Vitamin E

Nyombeka

is a local delicacy among the northerners in Ghana. NativeNosh nyombeka is a vegan and vegetarian-friendly dish, as it does not contain any animal products. It is also gluten-free, because it is made with corn flour and beans leaf and is typically served in a clay pot. The dish is green in color and is often served with a side of rice or other starches. Nyombeka is a rich source of protein, fiber, iron, and vitamin A. The beans leaf or cowpea leaf that is made of the nyombeka is a nutritious legume that provides folate, thiamine, vitamin C, and vitamin E3. It is often prepared for special occasions, such as festivals, weddings, and funerals showing a symbol of hospitality and friendship, as it is shared among family and guests. NativeNosh Nyombeka is a delicious and healthy dish that showcases the culinary diversity and culture of Ghana.

About

  • Mision

    Our mission is two-fold: to delve into the intricacies of local Ghanaian foods and enhance their processing methods, and to showcase the nutritional richness of these foods while celebrating the vibrant culture they represent. This multifaceted approach not only aligns with NativeNosh Foods' commitment to quality but also opens up new avenues for growth and impact in the region.

  • Nutritional Excellence

    Ghanaian foods are not just delicious; they are also nutritional powerhouses. We aim to highlight the health benefits of these traditional dishes, emphasizing their rich nutrient content and unique contribution to a balanced diet. Through collaborations with nutritionists and health experts, we will develop informative content that educates consumers on the nutritional advantages of incorporating Ghanaian foods into their daily lives.

  • Research and Innovation

    Our team of enthusiastic researchers will engage with local communities to understand the traditional methods of food preparation, the unique ingredients used, and the cultural significance of each dish. We will collaborate closely with local chefs and food experts, combining their invaluable knowledge with modern scientific insights to optimize processes for efficiency and quality. Our goal is to preserve the authenticity of Ghanaian cuisine while introducing innovations that streamline production and ensure the highest standards.
